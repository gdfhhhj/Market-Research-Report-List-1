<p><p>Ferric Sulphate, also known as iron(III) sulfate, is an inorganic compound commonly used as a coagulant in wastewater treatment processes. It is a pale yellow solid, soluble in water, and has various industrial applications. Ferric Sulphate is primarily used as a flocculating agent to remove solid particles from water and facilitate the separation of suspended impurities. It is widely employed in industries such as electronics, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, and paper and pulp.</p><p>The current outlook for the Ferric Sulphate Market is optimistic, with steady growth projected in the coming years. The increasing demand for clean water and stringent government regulations regarding wastewater treatment are the primary drivers of market growth. Additionally, the growth of industrial sectors and the rising population contribute to the increasing need for effective water treatment solutions, subsequently boosting the demand for ferric sulphate.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is expected to witness significant growth due to the surging adoption of ferric sulphate as a substitute for traditional coagulants such as aluminum sulfate and ferric chloride. Ferric sulphate offers several advantages, including better performance, cost-effectiveness, and minimal environmental impact, making it a preferred choice for water treatment processes.</p><p>The Ferric Sulphate Market is anticipated to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.6% during the forecast period.
