<p><p>Electrodeposition, also known as electroplating or electrophoretic deposition, is a process wherein a metal coating is deposited onto an object using an electric current. It is widely used for decorative purposes, corrosion resistance, and improving the physical and mechanical properties of surfaces. Electrodeposition finds applications in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, electronics, jewelry, and construction.</p><p>The electrodeposition market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing demand for corrosion-resistant coatings and the growing automotive industry. With the rising concerns about corrosion damage in various sectors, the need for durable and protective coatings has surged, driving the market for electrodeposition. Additionally, the automotive industry's adoption of electroplating processes to enhance the appearance, durability, and corrosion resistance of components further adds to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in electrodeposition technology and the development of new electroplating materials are expected to propel market growth.
