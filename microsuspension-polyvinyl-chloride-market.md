<p><p>Microsuspension polyvinyl chloride (PVC) is a type of PVC that is produced through a suspension polymerization process. It is available in two different market types: homogeneous type and heterogeneous type. </p><p>The homogeneous type refers to PVC that has a uniform particle size distribution, resulting in a smooth and consistent appearance. This type is typically used in applications where visual aesthetics are important, such as in the production of films, sheets, and containers.</p><p>On the other hand, the heterogeneous type of microsuspension PVC has a varied particle size distribution, leading to a more textured and uneven appearance. This type is often used in applications where mechanical properties and impact resistance are crucial, such as in pipes, fittings, and profiles.</p></p>
