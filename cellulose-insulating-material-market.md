<p><p>Cellulose insulating materials are categorized into two main types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers are derived from plants, such as cotton, hemp, and flax. These fibers have excellent insulation properties and are considered renewable and environmentally friendly. On the other hand, man-made cellulose fibers are produced from chemically treated wood pulp, such as viscose and rayon. These fibers offer high insulation performance and are used in various applications. Both natural and man-made cellulose fibers contribute to the growing market of cellulose insulating materials.</p></p>
