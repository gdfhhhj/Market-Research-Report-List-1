<p><p>Virtual microscopy is a technology that involves digitizing microscopic slides and enabling their examination in a virtual environment. Brightfield microscopy involves illuminating samples with white light and observing them in their natural colors, making it suitable for routine examination. Fluorescence microscopy, on the other hand, uses specific wavelengths of light to excite fluorescent dyes in samples, allowing for the visualization of specific structures or molecules. Both types of microscopy are widely used in research, education, and diagnostics for their ability to provide detailed and high-resolution images for analysis.</p></p>
