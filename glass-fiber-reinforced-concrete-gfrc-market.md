<p><p>Glass Fiber Reinforced Concrete (GFRC) is a composite material made up of a cementitious matrix and glass fibers. GFRC is commonly used in architectural facades, cladding panels, decorative elements, and precast products.</p><p>The future outlook for the GFRC market is promising. <p><p>Glass Fiber Reinforced Concrete (GFRC) is a composite material consisting of a cementitious matrix reinforced with glass fibers. The market is categorized into three types: sprayed, premix, and hybrids. Sprayed GFRC involves the application of GFRC spray mix onto molds or structures. Premix GFRC refers to the precast elements created by mixing glass fibers and dry materials, subsequently adding water on-site. Hybrid GFRC combines traditional GFRC with elements like polyvinyl alcohol fibers, steel fibers, or carbon fibers. These variants offer different application methods, reinforcing fibers, and characteristics to cater to diverse construction needs.</p></p>
