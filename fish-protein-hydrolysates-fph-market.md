<p><p>Fish Protein Hydrolysates (FPH) are derived from the enzymatic hydrolysis of fish proteins, resulting in the breakdown of large protein molecules into smaller peptides and amino acids. The FPH market can be categorized into two types: High Purity and Low Purity. High Purity FPH undergoes extensive processing to remove impurities, resulting in a higher concentration of protein content and better solubility. This type is preferred in the pharmaceutical and sports nutrition industries. On the other hand, Low Purity FPH is less processed, containing higher levels of impurities, and is commonly used in animal feed and fertilizers.</p></p>
