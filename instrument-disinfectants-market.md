<p><p>Instrument disinfectants can be classified into two types - alcohol-based and non-alcohol based. Alcohol-based disinfectants typically contain ethanol or isopropyl alcohol, which are effective in killing a broad spectrum of microorganisms. These products are commonly used in healthcare settings due to their rapid action and broad antimicrobial activity. On the other hand, non-alcohol disinfectants do not contain alcohol and instead utilize alternative active ingredients such as hydrogen peroxide or quaternary ammonium compounds. These types of disinfectants are often preferred in environments where alcohol-based products may be hazardous or undesirable.</p></p>
