<p><strong>The Electronic Stablity Program (ESP) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Dual-channel System</li><li>Three-channel System</li><li>Four-channel System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electronic Stability Program (ESP) is an automotive safety feature that helps maintain vehicle stability and prevent skidding during sudden maneuvers. There are three different market types for ESP systems - dual-channel, three-channel, and four-channel systems. Dual-channel ESP utilizes sensors on two wheels to monitor vehicle stability. Three-channel systems monitor stability on three wheels, usually the two front wheels and one rear wheel. Four-channel systems, considered the most advanced, monitor stability on all four wheels individually. Depending on the vehicle's requirements, manufacturers choose the appropriate ESP system to enhance safety and control.</p></p>
