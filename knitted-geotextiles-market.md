<p><p>Knitted geotextiles are a type of synthetic fabric used in civil engineering and construction projects for their superior strength and durability. There are four main types of knitted geotextiles that dominate the market: Polypropylene (PP), Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET), Polyamide (PA), and High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE). PP knitted geotextiles offer excellent chemical resistance, dimensional stability, and resistance to UV degradation. PET geotextiles are known for their high tensile strength, elongation properties, and resistance to biological degradation. PA geotextiles provide exceptional strength, durability, and abrasion resistance, while HDPE geotextiles exhibit excellent resistance to chemicals, moisture, and UV radiation, making them suitable for exposed environments.</p></p>
