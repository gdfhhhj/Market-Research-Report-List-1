<p><p>Aviation gasoline, also known as avgas, is a specialized type of fuel used in piston-engine aircraft. It differs from automotive gasoline (mogas) due to its higher octane rating and lower volatility, which are essential for safe and efficient aircraft operation. Avgas is currently produced and supplied by a limited number of refineries worldwide.</p><p>The aviation gasoline market has witnessed steady growth over the years due to the increasing number of general aviation aircraft, primarily in emerging economies. The demand for avgas is driven by factors such as the expansion of air travel, growth in air cargo transportation, and the rise in recreational flying activities.</p><p>Market analysis suggests that the aviation gasoline market is expected to continue its growth trajectory during the forecast period at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.9%. This growth can be attributed to several trends in the market:</p><p>1. Increasing fleet size: The growing number of general aviation aircraft, including business jets and light aircraft, is driving the demand for avgas. This trend is particularly prominent in emerging economies where demand for personal air travel and recreational flying is rising.</p><p>2. Infrastructure development: Investments in aviation infrastructure, such as the construction of new airports and expansion of existing ones, are fueling the demand for avgas. These developments support the growth of the aviation industry, leading to increased fuel consumption.</p><p>3. Technological advancements: Manufacturers are investing in research and development to improve the efficiency of piston-engine aircraft. This includes the development of engines that can run on mogas, which is cheaper and more easily available than avgas. However, the transition to mogas is a gradual process, and avgas will continue to dominate the market in the short to medium term.</p><p>4. Environmental concerns: The aviation industry, including the general aviation sector, is under increasing pressure to reduce its environmental impact. This has led to the exploration of alternative fuels with lower carbon emissions. However, the aviation gasoline market is expected to maintain steady growth as it will take time for sustainable aviation fuels to become widely available and economically viable.</p><p>In conclusion, the aviation gasoline market is expected to grow steadily at a CAGR of 4.9% during the forecast period. Factors such as increasing fleet size, infrastructure development, technological advancements, and environmental concerns are driving the demand for avgas in the aviation industry. While the industry is exploring alternative fuels, avgas will continue to dominate the market for the foreseeable future.</p></p>

<p><p>Aviation Gasoline (Avgas) is classified into three market types based on their octane rating and lead content. Avgas 80/87 is the lowest octane rating fuel, suitable for low-compression piston engines. Avgas 100/130 is a high-performance fuel with a greater octane rating, ideal for high-compression piston engines. Lastly, Avgas 100LL (Low Lead) is the most commonly used aviation gasoline, designed for modern high-performance engines. With reduced lead content for environmental reasons, it ensures compatibility with older engines while providing adequate performance.</p></p>
