<p><p>Silicones are a diverse class of polymers with various applications in different markets. Silicones can be categorized into different types including elastomers, fluids, resins, gels, and others. Elastomers are flexible materials with excellent heat resistance and elasticity, commonly used in rubber applications. Fluids are low viscosity silicone liquids used as lubricants, heat transfer agents, or hydraulic fluids. Resins are high molecular weight silicones used in encapsulation, adhesives, or coatings. Gels have a semi-solid consistency and find application in skincare products or as fillers. Other silicone markets include sealants, mold-making materials, and electrical insulators.</p></p>
