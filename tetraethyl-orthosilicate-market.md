<p><p>Tetraethyl Orthosilicate (TEOS) is primarily used in the fabrication of silicon dioxide films, which find wide applications in electronics and optoelectronics industries. The market for TEOS can be classified into two types: the direct method and the STC (Silicon Tetrachloride) method. In the direct method, TEOS is synthesized directly from silicon (Si) and ethanol (C2H5OH). Whereas, in the STC method, TEOS is synthesized using silicon tetrachloride (SiCl4) and ethanol. Both methods offer varying advantages, such as cost-effectiveness and higher purity, depending on the specific requirements of the end-use industries.</p></p>
