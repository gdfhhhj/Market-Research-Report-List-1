<p><p>In the context of hip prosthesis market types, Type I refers to primary hip prostheses, which are used in the first-time replacement of a damaged hip joint. These prostheses are the most commonly used and offer good longevity and functional outcomes. On the other hand, Type II represents revision hip prostheses, used in cases where a previous hip replacement has failed or requires improvement. Revision prostheses are designed to address the specific needs of patients with complex hip joint conditions, such as bone loss or implant loosening.</p></p>
