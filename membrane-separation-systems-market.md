<p><p>Membrane separation systems are used to separate different components of a mixture based on the size and charge of particles. Microfiltration membranes remove larger particles, while ultrafiltration membranes separate smaller ones. Nanofiltration allows for the separation of ions and molecules, and reverse osmosis removes almost all contaminants. Chromatography uses a stationary phase to separate components based on their affinity to the stationary phase. Ion exchange involves the exchange of ions between a resin and the solution to separate different ions. These techniques find applications in various industries, including water treatment and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
