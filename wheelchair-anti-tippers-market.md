<p><p>Wheelchair anti-tippers are devices designed to prevent wheelchair tipping incidents, ensuring user safety. Forward anti-tippers are attached to the front of the wheelchair and provide stability when moving forward or navigating slopes. They prevent the wheelchair from leaning too far forward and tipping over. Rear anti-tippers, on the other hand, are installed at the rear end of the wheelchair to prevent backward tipping. They help maintain balance and prevent accidents when reversing or navigating downhill slopes. Both types of anti-tippers play a critical role in enhancing wheelchair stability and user confidence.</p></p>
