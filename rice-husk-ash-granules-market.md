<p><p>Rice Husk Ash (RHA) is obtained from the burning of rice husks, which are the outer protective layers of rice grains. Rice husks are a byproduct of rice milling and consist of silica, carbon, and other organic materials. The ash obtained after burning the husks is granulated to form Rice Husk Ash Granules.</p><p>Rice Husk Ash Granules have various applications in industries such as construction, agriculture, ceramics, and refractories. The granules can be used as a partial replacement for cement in concrete production, leading to improved strength and durability. They also have insulating properties and are used in the manufacturing of bricks, blocks, and tiles. In agriculture, they are used as a soil amendment to improve fertility and water retention.</p><p>The Rice Husk Ash Granules Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing demand for sustainable construction materials is a major driving factor for the market growth. RHA granules not only improve the performance of concrete but also reduce CO2 emissions.
