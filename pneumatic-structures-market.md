<p><p>Pneumatic structures refer to structures that utilize pressurized air for their stability and shape. The market for such structures can be categorized into two types: air-supported structures and air-inflated structures. Air-supported structures are large, flexible membranes that are supported by a continuous flow of air, creating a stable and aerodynamic space. Air-inflated structures, on the other hand, consist of air-filled panels or cushions that form the structure when inflated. Both types of structures offer unique advantages such as quick installation, energy efficiency, and versatility in use.</p></p>
