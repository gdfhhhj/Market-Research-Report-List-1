<p><p>Motor Control Centers (MCC) are integrated assemblies used to control and distribute electrical power for industrial motors. They provide a centralized platform for monitoring and controlling various motor-driven devices in a manufacturing or processing facility. MCCs consist of several motor starters, variable frequency drives, and other control devices, enclosed in a protective enclosure with operator interface panels.</p><p>The Motor Control Centers (MCC) market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory. The increasing demand for energy-efficient and reliable motor control systems, growing industrial automation, and the need for improved process control and monitoring are the key factors driving the market growth.
