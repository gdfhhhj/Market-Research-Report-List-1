<p><p>The automotive emission control system market consists of various types of components such as the oxygen sensor, EGR (exhaust gas recirculation) valve, catalytic converter, air pump, PCV (positive crankcase ventilation) valve, and charcoal canister. The oxygen sensor measures the oxygen levels in the exhaust gases, while the EGR valve recirculates a portion of the exhaust gas back into the engine. The catalytic converter turns harmful emissions into less harmful gases. The air pump helps with oxygenation, and the PCV valve controls the release of gases from the engine. The charcoal canister absorbs and stores fuel vapors emitted from the fuel tank.</p></p>
