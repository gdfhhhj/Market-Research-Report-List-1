<p><p>Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer (EPDM) is a synthetic rubber elastomer made from ethylene, propylene, and a diene monomer, usually 5-ethylidene-2-norbornene (ENB). EPDM has a unique molecular structure that provides exceptional resistance to heat, ozone, weather, and aging. It is widely used in various applications such as automotive, construction, electrical, roofing, and others.</p><p>The EPDM market is experiencing significant growth due to its excellent properties and increasing demand from end-use industries. The automotive sector is one of the major consumers of EPDM, driven by the growing demand for lightweight and fuel-efficient vehicles. EPDM is used in seals, gaskets, weather-stripping, and other automotive components.</p><p>In the construction industry, EPDM is extensively used in roofing membranes, as it offers excellent resistance to UV radiation, weathering, and water. The increasing construction activities, especially in emerging economies, are boosting the demand for EPDM in this sector.</p><p>Additionally, the electrical industry is also a key consumer of EPDM due to its insulating properties. EPDM is used in cables, wires, and other electrical components to provide insulation and protection against electrical shocks.</p><p>The market analysis suggests that the EPDM market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13% during the forecast period. <p><p>Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer (EPDM) is a synthetic rubber compound used in various applications, including automotive parts, electrical insulation, and roofing membranes. EPDM is produced through two primary methods: solution polymerization and suspension polymerization. </p><p>In solution polymerization, the monomers are dissolved in a solvent, which enables better control over molecular weight and polymer structure. This method is efficient for producing high-quality EPDM with consistent properties. </p><p>Suspension polymerization involves dispersing the monomers in a water-based medium, allowing for the formation of small polymer particles. This method is cost-effective and suitable for producing EPDM with good processability and physical properties.</p></p>
