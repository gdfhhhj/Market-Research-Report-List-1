<p><p>Trichlorosilane is a chemical compound widely used in the solar photovoltaic industry for the production of silicon-based solar cells. There are two commonly employed processes in the market for producing trichlorosilane: the Direct Chlorination (DC) process and the Hydrochlorination (HC) process. In the DC process, trichlorosilane is formed by reacting silicon with chlorine gas directly, while in the HC process, silicon tetrachloride is hydrochlorinated to produce trichlorosilane. Both processes play a crucial role in the manufacturing of solar cells, contributing to the growth of the solar photovoltaic market.</p></p>
