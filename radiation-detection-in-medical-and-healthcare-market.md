<p><p>Radiation detection in the medical and healthcare market encompasses three main types of detectors: gas-filled detectors, scintillators detectors, and solid-state detectors. Gas-filled detectors operate by measuring the ionization produced when radiation interacts with the gas. Scintillators detectors use special crystals that emit light when exposed to radiation, which is then measured. Solid-state detectors rely on semiconductor materials that produce an electric current when radiation is absorbed. These detectors play a crucial role in detecting and monitoring radiation exposure in medical and healthcare settings, ensuring the safety of patients and healthcare professionals.</p></p>
