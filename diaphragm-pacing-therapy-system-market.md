<p><p>The Diaphragm Pacing Therapy System is a medical treatment for patients with chronic respiratory insufficiency. It includes two main types: the External Diaphragm Pacemaker and the Diaphragm Pacemaker. The External Diaphragm Pacemaker uses electrodes on the skin to stimulate the diaphragm muscles and improve breathing. On the other hand, the Diaphragm Pacemaker is surgically implanted and connected to the phrenic nerves, providing electrical stimulation to the diaphragm. Both devices aim to assist patients with respiratory difficulties, enhancing their quality of life and reducing the reliance on mechanical ventilators.</p></p>
