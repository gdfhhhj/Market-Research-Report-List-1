<p><p>Automotive flock refers to the process of applying short fibers to the surface of automotive components such as dashboards, door panels, and trim. These fibers are usually made of nylon, viscose, or polyester and are electrostatically charged and applied to adhesive-coated surfaces, creating a velvety texture. The flocking process enhances the aesthetics, functionality, and durability of automotive interiors by providing an anti-glare, anti-scratch, and anti-slip surface.</p><p>The future outlook of the automotive flock market is promising, with a projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.4% during the forecasted period.
