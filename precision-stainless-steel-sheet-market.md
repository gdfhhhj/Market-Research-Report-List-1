<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The precision stainless steel sheet market is divided into two types: cold drawn and hot rolled. Cold drawn refers to the process of stretching stainless steel at room temperature to form thin and smooth sheets, which are known for their higher dimensional accuracy and improved surface finish. On the other hand, hot rolled involves heating stainless steel above its recrystallization temperature and then passing it through rollers, resulting in larger and thicker sheets. These sheets have a rougher surface but are more cost-effective and have higher tensile strength than cold drawn sheets.</p></p>
